

## Recommendation Memo
## Executive Summary
The company tested a new onboarding and activation campaign by comparing a Treatment group
against the existing onboarding experience (Control group).
The primary objective was to improve user activation and conversion while maintaining acceptable
levels of customer experience and operational efficiency.
Analysis indicates that the Treatment experience improves user engagement and activation metrics.
However, some guardrail metrics require monitoring before a full rollout decision is made.
## North Star Metric
## Selected North Star Metric
## Trial Start Rate
## Why This Metric Was Selected
Trial Start Rate directly measures whether users successfully reach the activation stage after
onboarding.
This metric is closely connected to:
User acquisition efficiency
Product activation
Future paid conversion opportunities
Long-term revenue growth
A higher Trial Start Rate indicates that more users are progressing into meaningful product usage.

## Result
The observed improvement favors the Treatment group.
The evidence suggests that the Treatment onboarding experience improves activation performance.
Business interpretation:
Users exposed to the new onboarding flow are more likely to begin product adoption activities than
users exposed to the existing onboarding experience.
## Guardrail Analysis
The recommendation was not based solely on conversion improvement.
Several guardrail metrics were evaluated.
## Support Ticket Rate
Treatment generated a higher support ticket rate than Control.
Potential implications:
Increased customer support workload
Additional operational costs
Possible onboarding friction for some users
## Refund Rate
Refund behavior should continue to be monitored during rollout.
No critical risk was identified, but ongoing tracking is recommended.
Average Days to Convert
Treatment users converted more quickly than Control users.
This is a positive business outcome because value realization occurs sooner.

Increased support demand may require operational planning.
