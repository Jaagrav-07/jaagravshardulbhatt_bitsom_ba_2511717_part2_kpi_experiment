# Recommendation Memo

# Executive Summary

The company conducted an A/B experiment to evaluate a new onboarding and activation experience by comparing a **Treatment** group with the existing **Control** group. The primary objective was to improve user activation, engagement, and conversion while maintaining customer satisfaction and operational efficiency.

The analysis indicates that the Treatment onboarding experience significantly improves user activation and engagement metrics. Although the experiment also revealed a slight increase in customer support requests, the overall business impact remains positive. Therefore, a monitored rollout is recommended.

---

# North Star Metric

## Selected Metric

**Trial Start Rate**

### Why This Metric Was Selected

Trial Start Rate directly measures the percentage of users who successfully begin using the product after completing onboarding. It is closely linked to:

* User acquisition efficiency
* Product activation
* Future paid conversion opportunities
* Long-term revenue growth

A higher Trial Start Rate indicates that more users are progressing into meaningful product usage, making it the most appropriate success metric for this experiment.

# Experiment Results

## Landing Page Visit Rate

The Treatment group recorded a higher landing page visit rate than the Control group, indicating stronger user interest and acquisition quality.

## Trial Start Rate

The Treatment group achieved a significantly higher Trial Start Rate, demonstrating improved onboarding effectiveness.

## Engagement Score

Users exposed to the Treatment experience showed higher engagement levels, suggesting increased interaction with the product.

## Average Days to Convert

Treatment users converted faster than those in the Control group, reducing the time required for users to realize product value.

## Revenue Metrics

Revenue-related indicators showed a positive improvement for the Treatment group, suggesting stronger long-term business potential.

---

# Hypothesis Test Interpretation

## Null Hypothesis (H₀)

There is no significant difference between the Trial Start Rates of the Control and Treatment groups.

## Alternative Hypothesis (H₁)

The Trial Start Rate of the Treatment group is significantly higher than that of the Control group.

## Result

The experimental results support the alternative hypothesis. The Treatment onboarding experience resulted in higher activation performance compared to the existing onboarding process.

### Business Interpretation

Users who experienced the new onboarding flow were more likely to begin using the product, leading to improved activation, engagement, and conversion outcomes.

---

# Guardrail Analysis

The rollout recommendation was based not only on improved conversion metrics but also on careful evaluation of business risk indicators.

## Support Ticket Rate

The Treatment group generated a higher support ticket rate than the Control group.

Potential implications include:

* Increased customer support workload
* Higher operational costs
* Possible onboarding friction for certain users

## Refund Rate

Refund behavior remained within acceptable limits. Although no significant risk was observed, continued monitoring is recommended during rollout.

## Average Days to Convert

Treatment users converted more quickly, enabling customers to realize product value sooner.

## Engagement Score

Higher engagement scores suggest improved customer satisfaction, stronger product adoption, and greater long-term retention potential.

---

# Segment-Level Insights

Performance varied across several customer segments, including:

* Region
* Device Type
* Traffic Source
* Subscription Plan

Although the Treatment outperformed the Control overall, the magnitude of improvement differed across segments. Future optimization efforts should focus on understanding these differences and improving underperforming segments.

---

# Final Recommendation

## Recommendation

**Proceed with a monitored rollout of the Treatment onboarding experience for all users.**

### Supporting Reasons

* Higher Trial Start Rate
* Increased user engagement
* Faster conversion time
* Positive revenue indicators
* Strong overall business performance

---

# Required Monitoring

The following metrics should be monitored continuously after deployment:

* Support Ticket Rate
* Refund Rate
* Customer Satisfaction Score (CSAT)
* Segment-specific performance
* Long-term retention and revenue metrics

---

# Risks and Limitations

* The experiment duration may not fully capture long-term retention behavior.
* Customer behavior may change after a full-scale rollout.
* Certain customer segments may respond differently to the Treatment experience.
* Increased customer support demand may require additional operational resources.

---

# Next Steps

1. Roll out the Treatment onboarding experience to all users.
2. Monitor guardrail metrics on a weekly basis.
3. Track long-term retention, engagement, and revenue quality.
4. Conduct follow-up A/B experiments to further optimize onboarding.
5. Review segment-level performance and implement targeted improvements where necessary.

---

# Final Conclusion

The Treatment onboarding experience demonstrates meaningful improvements in user activation, engagement, conversion speed, and overall business performance while maintaining acceptable operational risk. Based on the experimental evidence, the Treatment should be deployed with continuous monitoring of key guardrail metrics to ensure sustained success and identify opportunities for further optimization.
